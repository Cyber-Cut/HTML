Further Precautions to Reduce Risks

1. Client Induction

Undertaking a client induction makes you aware of site-specific staff movement patterns, walkways, and instructions, reducing incident risk.

2. Tool Management

Keep only currently-used tools nearby. Return unused equipment to designated storage to avoid slips, trips, falls, and spillages.

üìñ Safe System of Work Procedures

‚úì DO's

  • ALWAYS ensure you have the correct PPE
  • ALWAYS use protective gloves for spillages/breakages
  • ALWAYS use signage to mark your work area
  • ALWAYS seek assistance if unsure
  • ALWAYS return equipment clean and tidy
  • ALWAYS report all incidents
  • ALWAYS stick to warehouse/yard walkways
  • ALWAYS wear hi-vis vest, safety boots, ear defenders
  • ALWAYS apply the 4 meter separation rule in aisles

‚úó DON'Ts

  • NEVER undertake untrained tasks (e.g., COSHH)
  • NEVER leave equipment unattended (except in storage)
  • NEVER use mobile phone unless authorized
  • NEVER return damaged equipment without reporting
  • NEVER use non-BLUE colored equipment

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Pre-operation Inspection & PPE

  • Ensure all equipment is available and undamaged
  • Verify correct site-specific PPE
  • Minimum requirement: Hi-vis jacket and safety boots on all sites

Step 2: General Cleaning

  • Familiarize yourself with designated storage area
  • Use ISM Search and BLUE cleaning equipment only (cloth, bucket, mop, etc.)
  • Keep colored equipment separate from other colors
  • Locate waste bins and understand recycling policy

Step 3: Cleaning Within or Under Racking

  • Wear hard hats/bump caps when working under or behind racking
  • Use dust masks if necessary (available on request)
  • Mark work area with caution signs/beacons for forklift operators
  • Only use machines you're trained to operate
  • Verify pallet stability before removal
  • Ensure goods don't obstruct others
  • Return goods to correct locations after cleaning
  • Report damaged goods/pallets to line manager and site supervisor

Step 4: Rodent Activity

‚ö†Ô∏è If you encounter evidence of rodent activity (shredded material or droppings):
  • Inform your line manager immediately
  • Do NOT disturb the material
  • Do NOT clean the area unless trained to do so

Risk: Leptospirosis (Weil's disease) can cause kidney damage, meningitis, liver failure, respiratory problems, and death.

Step 5: Spillages/Breakages

  • Isolate immediately using caution or wet floor signs
  • Report suspected hazardous chemical spillages to supervisor/area manager
  • Use appropriate equipment (e.g., absorbent booms/pads) if trained
  • Keep signage in place until completely dry/removed
  • Report damaged goods causing/caused by spillage
  • Clean and return all equipment to storage after use

Step 6: Dealing with Hazardous Chemicals

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: Only trained personnel handle hazardous chemicals
  • Report incidents/spillages to ISM Supervisor or Area Manager
  • Isolate immediately using caution/wet floor signs
  • Recognize COSHH hazard symbols on goods labels
  • Refer to COSHH & COSHH ISM RA & SSOW for cleaning procedures

Step 7: Damages

  • Report damaged goods/pallets to client supervisor and ISM Supervisor/Area Manager
  • Isolate with caution signs if obstructing others
  • Follow client supervisor's instructions for damaged goods disposal (e.g., moving to returns area)

‚úÖ Training Validation

Knowledge Check Questions

To confirm your understanding, please answer the following questions:

Question 1

Where must you wear a hard hat/bump cap when cleaning?

Answer: When working under or behind racking, or in any area with low ceiling height.

Question 2

In the event of an incident on site, who would you report this to?

Answer: Your ISM Supervisor or Area Manager, and the client's supervisor on site.

Question 3

What is the first thing you must do if you see/create a spillage/breakage?

Answer: Isolate it immediately using caution signs or wet floor signs.

Question 4

What other safe systems of work relate to Warehouse Housekeeping? Have you read them?

Answer: COSHH & COSHH ISM RA & SSOW, and Manual Handling Pallets RA & SSOW.

Question 5

If you have finished using a piece of equipment and do not require it again, what should you do?

Answer: Return it in a clean and tidy state to its designated storage area.

Question 6

What might you need if working underneath/within the racking?

Answer: Bump cap/hard hat (mandatory), dust mask (if necessary), caution signs/beacons, and appropriate cleaning equipment.
